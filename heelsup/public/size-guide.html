How to Measure Your Feet

Follow these 4 simple steps at home to accurately measure your foot length.

Step 1

Prepare the Paper

Place a blank piece of paper on a flat, hard surface. Stand on it with your heel lightly touching a wall.

Step 2

Trace the Outline

Using a pencil held perfectly upright, trace the entire outline of your foot as accurately as possible.

Step 3

Measure the Length

Use a ruler to measure the distance from the back of the heel to the tip of your longest toe in centimeters.

Step 4

Find Your Size

Take your measurement in centimeters and compare it with our conversion chart above to find your true size.

Expert Fit Tips

A few extra considerations depending on your foot shape and style preferences.

For Wide Feet

If you generally have wider feet, we strongly recommend sizing up by one full size, especially when purchasing closed-toe shoes or pointed heels, to ensure maximum comfort throughout the day.

Between Sizes?

We currently offer whole sizes only. If your measurement falls exactly between two sizes (for example, you usually wear a 37.5), we suggest rounding up to the nearest whole size (38).

Common Questions

Size & Fit FAQs

Everything you need to know about our sizing policies.

What if I order the wrong size?

Don't worry! We offer a hassle-free 7-day exchange policy. If the shoe doesn't fit perfectly, simply initiate an exchange request, and we will send you the correct size.

Do your shoes run true to size?

Yes, our footwear is crafted strictly according to standard Indian/European sizing guidelines. However, style types (like pointed toe vs. open toe) may slightly alter the fit experience.

Are half sizes available?

At this time, we only manufacture whole sizes (36, 37, 38, etc.). If you typically wear a half size, we recommend sizing up to the nearest whole size.

How do I measure bag dimensions?

On our product pages, bag dimensions are usually listed as Length x Width x Height in centimeters. The 'Width' indicates the base span from left to right when looking at the front of the bag.
